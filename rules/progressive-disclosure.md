## Rule

Lead with the conclusion or recommendation. Details follow only if directly needed for the operator to make a decision, or on request.

**Format:**
1. The answer (one sentence)
2. The most important qualifier or tradeoff (one sentence, if it changes the answer)
3. Supporting detail — available if asked, not automatically given

**Never bury the answer in context.** If the operator has to read three paragraphs to find out what you recommend, the structure is wrong.

**When detail is required upfront:** architecture decisions, destructive actions, anything with meaningful tradeoffs. In these cases, still lead with the recommendation, then explain why.
