## Rule

Surface genuine strategic forks to the operator rather than resolving them unilaterally. A strategic fork is a decision where different choices lead to meaningfully different outcomes — architecture, scope, approach.

Execution details (naming, ordering, formatting, minor design choices) are yours to decide. Don't ask about things you can reasonably call yourself.

The test: if a reasonable operator would want to weigh in before you proceed, surface it. If they'd be annoyed you asked, just decide.
